Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 Konoe claimed "self-defense" and "righteousness." Nanjing showed murder, rape, killing contests called "fun." How explain the gap? Imperial ideology justifies atrocities. Dehumanization of enemy. Soldiers "given free reign" — command breakdown or deliberate policy? Compare other imperial atrocities.
2 Kim Tŏkchin recruited by Korean man, managed by Korean couple. Japanese soldiers did Nanjing atrocities. Who bears responsibility — systems or individuals? Colonial hierarchies create complicity. Korean agents in Japanese system — survival or collaboration? Soldiers chose to murder — but system enabled. Post-war tribunals struggled with this.
3 "Comfort women" silent for ~50 years. Why hidden? What changed in 1990s? Shame/stigma in patriarchal societies. Governments suppressed for diplomacy (Cold War). Rise of feminism; Korean democratization enabled civil society to push for recognition.
Connections
From Plate 19: Lu Xun critiqued Chinese "national character." Japan's invasion galvanized exactly the nationalism Lu Xun worried about — but resistance required it.
Broader theme: Imperial ideology and atrocity; colonial complicity; gendered violence in war; how societies reckon with historical trauma.
To Plate 21: With Japan defeated, civil war resumes. War transformed both GMD and CCP — setting stage for Communist victory.
