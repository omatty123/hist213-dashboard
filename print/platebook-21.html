Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How did Japan justify its empire, and how did that justification compare to Western imperialism? Japan claimed to liberate Asia from the West. But Japan's rule was equally (or more) brutal. The ideology was self-serving—Pan-Asianism served Japanese interests. Compare to Western 'civilizing mission' rhetoric.
2 Why did the comfort women remain silent for so long, and what changed in the 1990s? Shame, stigma, disbelief. Cold War politics muted criticism of Japan (US ally). Korean democratization and women's movements enabled testimony. Kim Hak-sun's 1991 testimony broke the silence.
Causes / Effects / Connections
From Plate 20: Plates 17-19 traced nationalism in Japan, Korea, China. Those nationalisms collided violently in this war.
Context: Wartime atrocities remain unresolved in East Asian international relations. Understanding this history is essential for understanding contemporary Japan-Korea-China tensions.
To Plate 22: Plate 22 examines Japan's postwar reconstruction under American occupation. How did Japan transform from militarist empire to pacifist democracy?
Notes
• The comfort women testimonies are disturbing. Warn students and provide context.
• This is sensitive material for Korean, Chinese, and Japanese students. Be prepared for strong reactions.
• Avoid false balance: The historical facts of comfort women system are established. Denialism exists but is not legitimate scholarship.
• Connect to contemporary disputes: comfort women statues, textbook controversies, Yasukuni Shrine visits.
• Consider showing clips from documentary 'The Apology' (2016) about surviving comfort women.
