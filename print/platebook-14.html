Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How was Tonghak similar to and different from the Taiping movement? Similarities: Both peasant-based, religious ideology, charismatic founders with revelations, social reform demands. Differences: Tonghak was "Eastern Learning" vs. Taiping's Christianity; Tonghak smaller but triggered international war; Taiping nearly overthrew dynasty while Tonghak never threatened Seoul; Tonghak survives today as Cheondogyo while Taiping was destroyed.
2 Did the Tonghak rebels doom their cause by triggering foreign intervention? The Jeonju Peace Agreement (Jun 11) ended the rebellion peacefully. Reform was possible. But foreign armies had already landed by then. The tragic irony: Tonghak wanted to "drive out foreign barbarians" but triggered the war that put Japan in control. Question: Was foreign intervention inevitable regardless, or did the rebellion accelerate it?
