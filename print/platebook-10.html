Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 "Let us suppose that foreigners brought opium into England..." ‚Äî Why didn't Lin's moral argument work? Lin's argument was logically sound, but power trumped logic. Letter was never delivered. Britain framed war as about "free trade." Having the better argument means nothing without power to enforce it.
2 The Treaty made China pay $6 million for destroyed opium. What does this reveal about "international law"? International law was what powerful states said it was. Payment frames Lin's action as theft, not law enforcement. Treaty never legalized opium‚ÄîBritain got everything without admitting what war was about.
