Synthesis

Your peers found the presentation clear (6/7), but the "More Than a Summary" score (4/7) is the key signal: they want more analysis, not just description. Everyone understood the dual-claim structure (PRC and ROC both claiming to represent China). The strongest course connections came from peers linking the One-China principle to Qing-era boundaries and Mao's wish to "complete" China. The biggest surprise was that China is Taiwan's largest trading partner, and one peer was struck that Taiwan also claims ownership of the entire PRC. Your peers' questions point toward where to push deeper: "What is the 'completeness' that PRC is looking for?" asks about the ideological dimension. "Is it a matter of when, or if?" reframes the argument into something urgent. "Does China really have much of a reason to keep its grip on Taiwan past wanting it in the Republic?" challenges the assumption. "Is China cultivating European relations problematic for Taiwan's sovereignty?" opens an international dimension. These are the analytical moves that would lift the presentation from summary into argument.
