Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 Tokchae: "They made me vice-chairman because I was a hardworking farmer, and one of the poorest." What does this say about how ordinary people experience political upheaval? Neither man chose his ideology. Tokchae stayed for his dying father; Songsam's family fled. The 38th parallel was drawn by colonels who'd never been to Korea. Most people aren't ideologues—they're caught in historical currents.
2 Why does "Cranes" end with birds soaring into the sky rather than a clear resolution? What does ambiguity achieve? Cranes can cross borders humans cannot. The ending mirrors Korea's division: no resolution, only suspension. Did Songsam release his friend or set up a shooting? Literature makes us feel impossibility.
3 Kim Chi-ha was sentenced to death for "Five Bandits." Why was satire so dangerous to the Park regime? Dictatorships survive criticism but not mockery. Kim's grotesque imagery—tycoons in banknote suits, generals filling rice sacks with sand—stripped away legitimacy. Using p'ansori claimed Korean tradition for opposition.
Connections
From Plates 22-23: Japan rebuilt under US occupation; China under Mao. Korea's path shaped by both—occupied by Japan until 1945, divided by Cold War, devastated by war involving Chinese troops.
Broader theme: Division creates parallel experiments. Neither Korean system served the people: North built totalitarianism, South built authoritarian developmental state. Democracy came to S. Korea only in 1987.
To Plate 25: Japan's postwar miracle. Compare development models: Korea under military dictatorship (Kim's target), Japan under one-party democracy. Both suppressed labor.
Teaching Notes
• Let literature work emotionally before analyzing. Read the ending of "Cranes" aloud—devastating.
• Crane symbolism: mate for life, migrate freely across borders. DMZ is now nature sanctuary.
• Show map: Seoul only 35 miles from DMZ. Students don't realize proximity.
• "Five Bandits" is vulgar by design—bodies, sex, excrement degrade the powerful.
• Connect to today: Park Geun-hye (Park's daughter) impeached 2017 for Samsung corruption.
