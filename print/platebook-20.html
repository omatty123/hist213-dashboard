"Women are the slaves of slaves. The liberation of women requires the destruction of the family, private property, and the state." — He Zhen
Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How did Chinese revolutionaries connect gender oppression to other forms of oppression? He Zhen saw women's oppression as rooted in family/property/state. Feet binding, arranged marriage, lack of education all connected. Communist women's policies (marriage reform, education) followed this logic.
2 Why did the Communists win the civil war? Multiple factors: peasant mobilization through land reform, Nationalist corruption and inflation, Japanese war weakened GMD more than CCP, Mao's guerrilla strategy. Not inevitable—GMD had advantages too.
Causes / Effects / Connections
From Plate 19: Plate 19's Lu Xun critiqued Chinese 'national character.' Revolutionaries sought to transform it through social revolution—including gender revolution.
Context: Revolution is never just political—it involves transforming social relations, including gender. Compare Chinese Communist gender politics to Soviet, or to Meiji Japan's approach.
To Plate 21: Plate 21 examines the Pacific War—which interrupted China's civil war and reshaped all of East Asia.
Notes
• He Zhen is less famous than male revolutionaries but represents important strand of thought.
• Students may expect 'revolution = Communism.' Show the diversity of revolutionary visions (anarchist, feminist, nationalist).
• The civil war is complex—avoid simple 'good guys vs. bad guys' framing.
• Gender dimension often overlooked in political history. He Zhen reading corrects this.
