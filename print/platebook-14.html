The Rebellion's Path: January - November 1894
Key Terms
Person
Ch'oe Cheu 최제우
Tonghak founder (1824-1864). Received divine revelation 1860. Executed for heterodoxy.
Person
Jeon Bong-jun 전봉준
"General Green Bean" — Led peasant army. 152cm tall, 300K soldiers. Executed 1895.
Person
Jo Byeong-gap 조병갑
Corrupt Gobu magistrate. His illegal taxation sparked the uprising.
Place
Gobu 고부
Where uprising began Jan 1894. Peasants stormed county office.
Place
Jeonju 전주
Provincial capital captured Apr 1894. Peace Agreement signed Jun 11.
Place
Gongju 공주
Site of final battle Nov 1894. ~30,000 peasants massacred by Japanese-backed forces.
Concept
Tonghak 東學
"Eastern Learning" — syncretic religion opposing "Western Learning." Core belief: humans are heaven.
Concept
In nae ch'on 인내천
"Human beings are heaven" — Revolutionary doctrine that every person, even slaves, carries divinity.
Concept
Jibgang-so 집강소
Peasant self-governance bureaus established after Jeonju Peace. Proto-democratic local councils.

The Tragic Irony
"We do not rise to overthrow the king. We rise to punish corrupt officials who suck the blood of the people, and to drive out the foreign barbarians who violate our land."
— Jeon Bong-jun, Proclamation of the Peasant Army, March 1894

What They Got
Triggered Japanese takeover

The Big Question

The Jeonju Peace Agreement (June 11) ended the rebellion peacefully. Reform was possible. But by then, foreign armies had already landed. Did the Tonghak rebels doom their cause by triggering foreign intervention? Or were the great powers going to fight over Korea regardless?

Discussion Questions
1
How was Tonghak similar to and different from the Taiping Rebellion? (Both peasant-based, religious, reform demands — but Tonghak was "Eastern" vs. Taiping's Christianity)
2
The doctrine "in nae ch'on" (humans are heaven) challenged Korea's entire social hierarchy. Why was this revolutionary?
3
"Shrimp among whales" — Korea's position between China and Japan. Was there any way for Korea to escape this fate?
