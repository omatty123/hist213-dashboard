Who Wins China?谁得天下

Plate 20 / Feb 23 / EAST 213
It's 1925. China is broken into pieces. Three factions want power. None of them can hold it without the peasants, who are 80% of the population. Four crises will hit. Each faction picks A or B. After each crisis, the peasants vote: who do you trust now? Whoever holds peasant support at the end wins China.
Communists
Small. Disciplined. Radical. You promise land to the peasants, equality for women, the end of everything old. You need: believers.
Nationalists
You have the army, the cities, the banks, and foreign backing. Your coalition is landlords, merchants, generals. You need: order.
Warlords
You have guns and territory. Ideology is irrelevant. You answer to no one and you intend to keep it that way. You need: survival.
Peasants
You are 80% of China. You can't read. You own almost nothing. You want land, food, and for someone to stop burning your village. You are the jury. Your vote decides history.

Discussion: Look at the GMD's options across all four rounds. What's missing from every single one? Why couldn't they offer it? And what does He Zhen's reading tell us about what "revolution" actually meant to the people who fought for it?
Teacher Key: What Actually Happened
Round 1: 1925
CCP chose A. Mao broke with orthodox Marxism. Marx said the revolution belongs to urban workers. Mao looked at China and saw 400 million peasants. His 1927 Report on the Hunan Peasant Movement called the peasantry "a colossal event" that no force could hold back.
GMD chose A. Chiang Kai-shek built a modern army with Soviet (then Western) help. He launched the Northern Expedition and unified most of China by 1928. But he never touched the land question. He couldn't. His backers were the people who owned the land.
Warlords: mixed. Some cut deals, some fought. They fragmented over time.
Everyone else ignored 80% of the population. The CCP didn't.
Round 2: 1927
CCP chose A. After the Shanghai massacre, the CCP was nearly destroyed. Mao retreated to the Jinggang Mountains and built the Jiangxi Soviet. The Long March (1934-35) killed 90% of the marchers, but the survivors became the hardened core of a peasant revolutionary army.
GMD chose A. Chiang was obsessed: "First pacify the interior, then resist the foreign threat." He launched five encirclement campaigns against the Communists. Meanwhile, inflation and corruption hollowed out his government. He was a general running a country like an army.
Warlords mostly chose A. They aligned with the GMD for survival.
The GMD had every material advantage: army, money, international recognition. But they were a government of cities in a country of villages.
Round 3: 1937
CCP chose A. Communist guerrillas operated behind Japanese lines, organizing peasant resistance, distributing land, building local governments. By 1945 they controlled vast rural areas with millions of loyal supporters. The war was the best thing that ever happened to the CCP.
GMD chose A, then B. Chiang threw his best German-trained divisions at Japan in the Battle of Shanghai (1937). He lost 250,000 troops in three months. Then he retreated to Chongqing for eight years. His remaining army deteriorated into a corrupt, demoralized shell.
Some warlords chose B. Wang Jingwei's collaboration government in Nanjing was the most notorious case.
Japan destroyed the GMD's army. The CCP built a revolution behind enemy lines. The GMD fought the war. The CCP won the peace.
Round 4: 1945
CCP chose A. Peasants who received land during the war fought to keep it. The People's Liberation Army swelled with volunteers. Entire GMD divisions defected with their American weapons. By 1948 it was a rout.
GMD chose A. Chiang had American weapons but hollow troops. Drafted peasants with no stake in the outcome against volunteers defending their own land. Hyperinflation destroyed the urban middle class. By 1949, it was over.
Military hardware without popular legitimacy is an empty shell. The CCP won because they answered the one question that mattered to 400 million people: whose land is this?

He Zhen Connection

He Zhen argued in 1907 that revolution meant transforming everything: gender, family, property, the state. The CCP adopted some of her vision (marriage reform, women's rights, land redistribution) while abandoning the rest (anarchism, abolishing the state). They built the most powerful state in Chinese history in the name of destroying state power.

Ask the class: Did the CCP deliver on the revolution He Zhen imagined? What got included? What got left out? And who got to decide?
