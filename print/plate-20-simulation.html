Plate 20
Who Wins China?
Feb 23
The game: It's 1925. China is broken. Three factions compete for power, but none can win without the peasants, who are 80% of the population. Four crises will hit. Each faction chooses A or B. After each crisis, the peasants vote: who do you trust now? Whoever holds peasant support at the end wins China.
Communists (CCP)
Small, disciplined, radical. You promise total revolution: land to the peasants, equality for women, end of the old order.
Nationalists (GMD)
You have the army, the cities, and foreign support. Your backers are bankers, landlords, and foreign governments. You promise a modern, unified China.
Warlords
You have guns and territory. You don't care about ideology. You want to keep what's yours.
Peasants (the jury)
You are 80% of China. You can't read. You own almost nothing. You want: land, food, and someone to stop burning your village. After each round, you vote for whoever you trust most. Your vote decides history.

The real result: CCP proclaimed the People's Republic on October 1, 1949. The GMD fled to Taiwan.
Discussion: What did the CCP offer that nobody else did? Why wasn't military power enough for the GMD? What does He Zhen's reading tell us about what "revolution" actually meant?

Teacher Key — What Actually Happened

Round 1: 1925

CCP chose A — Mao Zedong broke with orthodox Marxism by going to the peasants instead of the urban proletariat. His 1927 Report on the Hunan Peasant Movement called the peasantry "a colossal event" that would sweep away all opposition.
GMD chose A — Chiang Kai-shek built a modern army with Soviet and later Western help. He unified much of China by force, but never touched the land question.
Warlords chose A/B — Some cut deals, some fought. They fragmented over time.
The CCP's decision to go to the countryside was the single most important strategic choice of the revolution. Everyone else ignored 80% of the population.

Round 2: 1927

CCP chose A — After the Shanghai massacre, Mao retreated to the Jinggang Mountains and built the Jiangxi Soviet. The Long March (1934-35) nearly destroyed them, but the survivors became the core of a peasant army.
GMD chose A — Chiang obsessed over destroying the CCP ("first pacify the interior, then resist the foreign threat"). He launched five encirclement campaigns. Meanwhile, inflation and corruption ate away at his base.
Warlords mostly chose A — They aligned with the GMD for survival.
The GMD had every advantage: army, money, international recognition. But they were a government of cities in a country of villages.

Round 3: 1937

CCP chose A — Communist guerrillas operated behind Japanese lines, organizing peasant resistance, distributing land, building local governments. By 1945 they controlled vast rural areas with millions of supporters.
GMD chose both A then B — Chiang threw his best German-trained divisions at Japan in Shanghai (1937), losing 250,000 troops. Then retreated to Chongqing. His remaining army deteriorated.
Some warlords chose B — Wang Jingwei's collaboration government in Nanjing was the most notorious case.
The war was a gift to the CCP. Japan destroyed the GMD's army while the CCP built a peasant revolution behind enemy lines. The GMD fought the war; the CCP won the peace.

Round 4: 1945

CCP chose A — Peasants who received land during the war fought to keep it. The People's Liberation Army swelled with volunteers. Entire GMD divisions defected.
GMD chose A — Chiang used American weapons but couldn't hold territory. His soldiers were drafted, unfed, and demoralized. Hyperinflation destroyed the urban middle class. By 1949 it was over.
The lesson: military hardware without popular legitimacy is an empty shell. The CCP won because they answered the one question that mattered to 80% of China: whose land is this?

He Zhen Connection

He Zhen argued in 1907 that revolution meant transforming everything: gender, family, property, the state. The CCP adopted some of her vision (marriage reform, women's rights, land redistribution) while abandoning the rest (anarchism, abolishing the state). Ask students: Did the CCP deliver on the revolution He Zhen imagined? What got left out?
