Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 What is 'East Asia' and why study these three countries together? Geographic proximity, shared cultural heritage (Confucianism, Buddhism, Chinese script), historical interaction. But also: this is a Western category—Chinese, Koreans, and Japanese didn't necessarily see themselves as part of one 'region.'
2 Where is the center of the world? For most of history, China considered itself the center (Middle Kingdom). Europeans put themselves at the center. Maps reflect worldviews. Show Chinese vs. European world maps. Ask: Who decides where the center is?
Causes / Effects / Connections
From Plate 0: This is the first class — we're establishing foundations for the entire course
Context: The question 'What is East Asia?' will echo throughout the course. We'll see how Western imperialism, nationalism, and Cold War divisions reshaped regional identities.
To Plate 2: Plate 2 will explore what actually unites East Asia: language, religion, and culture. Plate 3 will ask the harder question: What is 'modernity' and was it invented by the West?
