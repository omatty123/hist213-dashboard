Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 "Heaven did not create men above men" — but the Meiji system kept the emperor above all. How do we reconcile Fukuzawa's egalitarianism with imperial ideology? Fukuzawa attacked samurai-commoner distinction, not emperor. The emperor was being INVENTED as sacred precisely BECAUSE old hierarchies were collapsing. New equality needed new sacredness above it.
2 The Charter Oath promised "deliberative assemblies" and "public discussion" — but Japan didn't get a parliament until 1890. Was the Oath a lie? The Oath's vagueness was strategic — it could mean democracy OR oligarchy depending on interpretation. Compare to "all men created equal" — founding rhetoric often precedes founding reality by decades.
3 Kido wrote: "Our people are no different from Americans... it is all a matter of education." What does this imply about how societies change? Racial explanations rejected; difference is institutional, not inherent. Education becomes key to national strength. Kume noted European prosperity was only 40 years old — Japan could catch up.
Connections
From Plate 11: Tokugawa Japan's encounter with West — unequal treaties, forced opening. Meiji Restoration was ONE response. Leaders began as xenophobes and became Westernizers.
Broader theme: Path from "civilization and enlightenment" to Asian empire was shorter than reformers imagined. Charter Oath promised democracy; delivered oligarchy.
To Plate 13: While Japan transformed, China faced Taiping Rebellion (20-30M dead). Japan's "success" vs. China's "failure" looks different when China was fighting for survival.
