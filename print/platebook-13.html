Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 Was the Taiping Rebellion a 'proto-revolutionary' movement or a religious cult? Taiping proposed radical reforms: land redistribution, gender equality, end of opium. Chinese Communists later claimed them as precursors. But the movement was also deeply religious and violent. How do we evaluate such complex movements?
2 Why did internal rebellion do more damage to China than foreign aggression? The Opium War killed perhaps 20,000; Taiping killed 20-30 million. Internal crisis was more devastating than imperialism. But they were connected: Opium War had weakened Qing legitimacy.
