Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 What contemporary East Asian issue interests you most, and what historical background would illuminate it? Encourage students to start with what interests them now, then dig into historical roots. China's rise → Frank's argument about Asian-centered world economy. Japan-Korea tensions → Imjin War, colonial period.
2 What makes a historical argument compelling? Evidence from primary sources, engagement with counter-arguments, clear thesis, connection to broader themes. Avoid mere chronology—make an argument about significance or causation.
