Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How does literature capture what historical analysis cannot? 'Cranes' makes division personal—two friends, one childhood, opposite armies. Statistics tell us millions died; stories tell us what that meant. Literature as historical source.
2 Why did Korea remain divided when Germany reunified? Korean War was hotter than Cold War; both regimes dug in. China's involvement. Lack of superpower agreement. Economic gap between North and South grew. Inertia of institutions.
Causes / Effects / Connections
From Plate 23: Plates 22-23 showed Japan and China's postwar paths. Korea's path was shaped by their rivalry and Cold War division.
Context: Division creates parallel experiments: same people, opposite systems. Korea is the clearest case. Compare East/West Germany, China/Taiwan.
To Plate 25: Plate 25 examines Japan's postwar rise. Compare Korean and Japanese development models.
