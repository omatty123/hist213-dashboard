Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 The 1960 White Paper celebrates "values of frugality gave way to enjoying life." Is this progress? What gets lost? Does consumption = wellbeing? The document admits demand may be "created by producers through advertising." Japan shifted from saving to spending in one generation.
2 Takamure Itsue: feminist who supported fascism as "the war is ours, for women." How did maternalism lead to nationalism? Trace: maternalist feminism → cultural particularism → anti-Westernism → nationalism → fascism. Movements can be captured when they define against "the West."
3 As we conclude: what have we learned about "modernity" in East Asia? Return to Plate 3's questions. Modernity = liberation AND oppression. Consumer choice AND Minamata. Women's movements AND gender inequality. No single path. "Success" depends on who's counting.
Connections
From Plate 24: Korea's development learned from Japan (state-guided, export-oriented) but Japan's success bred complacency.
Broader theme: "Economic miracle" narrative obscures costs: Minamata victims, women, precarious workers. Success for whom?
Course arc: From Plate 3's "What is modernity?" to here: modernity meant different things, brought liberation AND oppression. Full circle.
