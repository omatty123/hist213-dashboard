Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 Was the Hundred Flowers a genuine attempt at liberalization or a trap? Scholars debate. Mao may have initially wanted criticism. But when criticism went further than expected, he reacted brutally. The result was traumatic: intellectuals learned never to speak honestly.
2 How do we evaluate Mao's legacy? Positive: unified China, land reform, women's rights, healthcare. Negative: tens of millions dead, Cultural Revolution destruction, authoritarian system. Chinese official line: '70% good, 30% bad.' Is this adequate?

Notes
• The Great Leap famine death toll is staggering. Spend time making this real—it's the worst famine in human history.
• 100 Flowers is a classic case of authoritarian manipulation. Students find it chilling.
• Mao's portrait still hangs on Tiananmen. The party hasn't fully repudiated him.
• Contemporary relevance: Xi Jinping has revived some Maoist elements (personality cult, ideological campaigns).
• Compare Chinese approach to history with Germany (confronting Nazi past) and Japan (limited reckoning).
