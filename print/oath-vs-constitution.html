TL;DR for discussion

Charter Oath: Revolutionary-sounding rhetoric, zero specifics. Compare to "all men are created equal" ‚Äî founding documents often promise more than they deliver.

Constitution: Created a parliament but kept real power with the emperor and oligarchs. The same men who wrote the Charter Oath decided what it "really meant" 21 years later ‚Äî and surprise, it meant they stayed in charge.

WORKED UNTIL IT FAILED

The Final Verdict

Japan modernized faster than any non-Western nation. Avoided colonization. Built an industrial economy and modern military. But: The same system that enabled rapid modernization also enabled unchecked militarism. No constitutional brake on military power ‚Üí invasion of neighbors ‚Üí 1945 catastrophe.

The Core Problem

The Meiji oligarchs wanted modernization without democratization. They got exactly what they designed: a strong state that could mobilize resources without popular consent. This worked brilliantly for catching up to the West ‚Äî and disastrously when the military decided to use that power.
