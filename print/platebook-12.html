Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 Was Meiji 'modernization' the same as 'Westernization'? Meiji leaders borrowed selectively: German constitution, British navy, French law. But they also consciously preserved 'Japanese spirit.' The slogan was 'Japanese spirit, Western technology' (wakon yōsai). Was this possible?
2 How should we evaluate Fukuzawa's 'Leaving Asia' today? It's both a product of its time and troublingly racist. Fukuzawa internalized Western hierarchies and applied them to other Asians. This thinking contributed to later Japanese imperialism. But he also championed equality and education.
Causes / Effects / Connections
From Plate 11: Plate 11 showed Japan's crisis upon Western arrival. Meiji Restoration was the response: transform or be colonized.
Context: Meiji Japan becomes the model for later Asian modernizers. But its 'success' also led to imperialism that devastated neighbors. 'Modernization' isn't always benign.
To Plate 13: Plate 13 examines China during the same period—the Taiping Rebellion nearly destroyed the Qing while Japan was transforming.
Notes
• Fukuzawa's 'Leaving Asia' is short—have students read it and react. Expect strong opinions.
• The Pacific Century documentary (linked in dashboard) has good footage on Meiji transformation.
• Show before/after images: samurai vs. conscript soldiers, Edo vs. gas-lit Tokyo.
• Avoid triumphalism: Meiji 'success' also created the war machine that attacked Asia.
• Contemporary connection: Japan's post-WWII recovery echoed Meiji patterns of state-led development.
