PRC Trade: The Global Picture

Is Trump's Trade War "China vs. the World"? Or Something Else Entirely?

The Question: China is in trade wars with the US, EU, Canada, and Australia.
But what about the other 190 countries?

The Big Picture: Two Different Worlds

China sells $1.6 trillion to the Global South vs. $1 trillion to the US + Western Europe combined.

The "trade war" is largely a Western bloc phenomenon. With the rest of the world, China is signing free trade agreements, upgrading partnerships, and eliminating tariffs.

RCEP: The World's Largest Trade Bloc

Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP) came into force in 2022. It includes:

  • 15 countries: China, Japan, South Korea, Australia, New Zealand + 10 ASEAN nations
  • 2.2 billion people (30% of world population)
  • $29.7 trillion GDP (30% of global GDP)
  • Trade with RCEP members: $1.74 trillion in 2023

Notable absence: The United States is not a member.

Source: Wikipedia: RCEP

The Trade Wars: A Western Bloc Phenomenon

1. US-China Trade War (2018–Present)

Ongoing

Before (2017): US tariff on China ~3%
Peak (April 2025): 145% / 125% both ways
Current (Jan 2026): 47.5% (US) / 31.9% (China)

WTO Status: China won 2020 ruling (DS543); US appealed to paralyzed Appellate Body. Both sides now operate outside WTO framework.

2. EU-China EV Dispute (2023–Present)

WTO Panel Active

EU: Up to 37.6% countervailing duties on Chinese EVs (Oct 2024)
China: Retaliated with duties on French brandy, probes into pork/dairy

3. Canada-China (2024–Present)

Escalating

Canada: 100% tariff on Chinese EVs
China: 100% on Canadian canola; exports collapsed 63%

4. Australia-China (2020–2024)

Resolved

206% wine tariffs removed March 2024. 80.5% barley tariffs removed August 2023. Triggered by Australia's call for COVID origin investigation.

The Pattern

All four trade conflicts are with Western-aligned democracies: US, EU, Canada, Australia. All are members of overlapping security/intelligence arrangements (Five Eyes, NATO, AUKUS, Quad).

China has zero active trade wars with Global South countries, despite trading more with them than with the West.

Historical Echo: The Canton System Inverted?

In the 18th century, the Qing restricted Western trade to a single port (Canton) while maintaining diverse relationships across Asia. The West saw this as obstruction; China saw it as managing troublesome partners.

Today, China faces tariff walls from the Western bloc while expanding trade everywhere else. The geography of conflict has a familiar shape: the same countries that forced China "open" in the 19th century are now trying to close it off.

Student question: Is the US-China trade war about "fair trade"—or about which power gets to set the rules of the global economy? Who benefits from framing this as "China vs. the World" when it's really "Western bloc vs. China"?

Key Takeaways for Discussion

  1. The "trade war" is geographically limited. China has active conflicts with ~5 Western-aligned countries and FTAs/partnerships with 150+ others.
  2. China is building an alternative architecture. RCEP, CAFTA 3.0, BRI, and bilateral FTAs create a trading system where the US is increasingly absent.
  3. The Global South is not neutral territory. China now sells 60% more to Global South ($1.6T) than to US + Western Europe combined ($1T).
  4. Critical minerals change the game. Unlike tariffs, mineral dominance cannot be easily reciprocated.
  5. Historical parallel: Just as the Opium War was about who controls the rules of trade, today's conflicts are about who writes the rules of the 21st-century economy.
