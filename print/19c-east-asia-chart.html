China

Population peaks at ~430 million around 1850, then crashes to 358 million by 1870. The Taiping Rebellion (1850-64) killed 20-30 million people. GDP per capita stagnates and slightly declines throughout the century.

Japan

Population grows steadily from 31 to 44 million. GDP per capita nearly doubles after the Meiji Restoration (1868), rising from $669 to $1,180 by 1900. Japan is the only one of the three whose economy clearly accelerates.

Korea

Population grows modestly from ~9 to ~15 million. GDP per capita shows slow growth. Korea's data is the least certain (Maddison marks it as an "outlier"). By 1900, Korea trails Japan's rapid rise but edges ahead of China.

Data: Maddison Project Database 2023 (Bolt & van Zanden), University of Groningen. GDP in 1990 international Geary-Khamis dollars. Korean estimates are less certain than China or Japan due to limited source records. Population figures for China 1850-1870 reflect catastrophic losses from the Taiping Rebellion and related conflicts.
