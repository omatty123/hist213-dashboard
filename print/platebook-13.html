Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 "The land is for all to till... Inequality shall exist nowhere." But also: "Nobody should keep private property." Liberation or totalitarianism? Both. Zeng Guofan: "The farmer cannot till his own land... the merchant cannot engage in trade for himself." Utopian promise, authoritarian reality.
2 Opium War: ~20,000 dead. Taiping: 20-30 MILLION. Why did internal rebellion kill more Chinese than foreign aggression? Foreign powers wanted trade, not conquest. Taiping threatened entire social order. "Not one surrendered" — religious war, fight to the death.
3 Hong believed himself Jesus's brother. "Fevered imaginings" or genuine faith? What made MILLIONS follow him? "Famine and economic depression... decline in dynastic prestige" after Opium War defeat. God Worshipers "proved better organized" in chaos. Desperation + meaning.
Connections
From Plate 12: Meiji Transformation (1868+). But TIMELINE: Taiping 1850-1864, Meiji 1868. Japan reformed AFTER China's civil war ended. Could ANY government reform while losing 20-30 million people?
Broader theme: Qing survived through provincial armies (Zeng Guofan). This SAVED dynasty but FRAGMENTED central power. Japan centralized; China decentralized.
To Plate 14: Tonghak Rebellion in Korea — another millenarian uprising with social reform demands. But Tonghak triggered Japanese intervention.
Teaching Notes
• DEATH TOLL: 20-30 million = more than WWI. Make this real.

• KEY QUOTE: "The land is for all to till" vs. "Nobody should keep private property"
