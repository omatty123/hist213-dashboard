08

Dejima & Tokugawa "Foreigners"FINAL PROJECT

A 120m x 75m artificial island in Nagasaki harbor — Japan's only official window to the West for 200 years (1641-1853). Dutch traders lived under strict surveillance. How did Tokugawa artists portray these "exotic" visitors? Kawahara Keiga's paintings offer a fascinating lens on cross-cultural perception, isolation policy, and controlled exchange.
