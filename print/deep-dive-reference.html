Socialist Realism

State-sanctioned art glorifying communist values through idealized workers, peasants, and revolutionary heroes. Dominant in USSR, China, and North Korea from 1930s-1980s. Modern echoes persist in North Korean media and some Chinese cinema.

WEIRD Bias in Psychology

Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic — 12% of humanity, yet 96% of psychology research subjects. East Asian societies show different patterns in self-concept, social cognition, and decision-making that challenge "universal" findings.

East Asian Geopower Rankings

Power is multidimensional. China leads military + manufacturing. Japan dominates tech + soft power. South Korea wins cultural exports. Rankings shift based on what you measure — the Lowy Institute tracks 8 dimensions across 27 countries.

North Korea's Paradox

Despite sanctions and isolation, GDP grew 3.7% in 2024. Pyongyang's skyline transformed since 2012 with "Pyonghattan" towers. Russia trade up 54%. TV ownership at 91%, refrigerators 36%. The regime adapts more than collapse narratives suggest.

05

Japan's Rice Fortress

778% tariff on imported rice. 2024 shortage sent prices up 92%. Food sovereignty argues nations can define their own agricultural policy — but Japan's overall food self-sufficiency is just 38%. Rice is cultural identity, not just calories.

GO DEEPER
→ TIME Why rice is a sticking point in US-Japan trade → Asia Pacific Foundation Subsidies, tariffs & climate change → Association for Asian Studies The Japan Rice Paradox → USDA (PDF) Rice Sector Policies in Japan
06

The Greater Bay Area

86 million people. 9 cities + Hong Kong + Macau. GDP exceeding ¥14.5 trillion. Shenzhen went from fishing village to "China's Silicon Valley" in 40 years. Designed to rival Tokyo Bay and San Francisco — testing "one country, two systems" limits.

Manchuria & Xinjiang

China's contested frontiers. Manchuria: Qing homeland → Japanese puppet state → industrial heartland. Xinjiang: Silk Road crossroads → Turkic-Muslim region → "new frontier." Both reveal how place, ethnicity, and empire collide across Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and indigenous memories.
