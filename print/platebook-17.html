Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How did Japanese nationalists adopt and adapt Western imperialist ideologies? Social Darwinism, racial hierarchy, civilizing mission, all borrowed from the West. But Japanese added Asian dimensions: Pan-Asianism, 'liberating' Asia from the West. The student becomes the teacher's rival.
2 Why did liberalism fail in Meiji Japan? Tokutomi started as a liberal. The Triple Intervention (1895) humiliated Japan and convinced many that only power mattered. International system rewarded aggression. Democracy seemed weak.
3 What does the Scramble for Concessions reveal about the international system Japan faced? After Japan returned Liaodong, Russia leased it anyway. Germany grabbed Jiaozhou, Britain took Weihaiwei. The lesson: the West punished Japan for doing exactly what Europeans did. Power, not rules.
Triple Intervention (1895): Russia, France, and Germany forced Japan to return the Liaodong Peninsula (won from China). Then Russia leased Port Arthur for itself (1898). This hypocrisy radicalized Japanese opinion.

Causes / Effects / Connections

Context: Nationalism often combines grievance (Triple Intervention) with ambition (empire). Understanding Japanese nationalism helps us understand nationalism's general dynamics—and dangers.
To Plate 18: Plate 18 examines Korean nationalism—forged in resistance to the Japanese imperialism we're studying today.
Notes
• Tokutomi lived until 1957—he saw Japan's rise, fall, and rebirth. A remarkable life spanning modern Japanese history.
• Avoid suggesting Japan was uniquely nationalist—every major power had similar ideologies. Japan learned from the West.
• The Triple Intervention is crucial for understanding the turn to nationalism. Japan played by Western rules and was punished anyway.
• Pan-Asianism was never really about liberating Asia—but some believed it. Self-deception is part of nationalism.
• Contemporary connection: Nationalist revivals in Japan today draw on similar rhetoric.
