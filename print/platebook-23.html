Penetrating Questions Key Points
1 Lu says "democratic liberties among the people" but "dictatorship" for "counter-revolutionaries." Who decides which category you belong to? The Party decides — and can reclassify you overnight. Critics who spoke in 1956 as "people" became "rightists" in 1957. The distinction enables arbitrary repression.
2 Why would intellectuals trust this invitation? Why did Mao issue it? Debate: genuine liberalization gone wrong, or calculated trap? The speech is ambiguous. Result traumatic either way — 550,000+ lives destroyed.
3 Lu criticizes "sectarianism" among swelled-headed Party members. What does this reveal about tensions within the CCP? Some Communists monopolized positions after victory. Lu wants broader mobilization — but on Party terms. Party always remains final arbiter.
Connections
From Plate 22: Japan rebuilt under American occupation with democratic constitution. China took revolutionary path — Party dictatorship with "democracy" redefined.
Broader theme: Revolutionary regimes need intellectuals but fear independent thought. Mobilization-then-repression defines Mao era.
To Plate 24: Korea divides; North follows Mao's model. Comparison illuminates what "people's democracy" meant in practice.
Teaching Notes
• READ CLOSELY: Lu's language reveals the trap. "Freedom for people, dictatorship for enemies" — but who decides?
• Rosenberg reference: Lu contrasts American "bourgeois freedom" with Chinese "people's freedom." Cold War matters.
• Lu himself later purged — the revolution devours its own.
• Ask: "If you were an intellectual in 1956, would you trust this? What signals would you look for?"
