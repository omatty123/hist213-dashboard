Penetrating Questions Very short answers
1 How does Korean nationalism differ from Japanese nationalism? Japanese nationalism was expansionist, based on power. Korean nationalism was defensive, based on victimhood and justice claims. Japanese cited Social Darwinism; Koreans cited civilization and righteousness. What are the implications of these different foundations?
2 Why did the March First Movement fail to achieve independence? International community didn't support; Wilson's self-determination didn't apply to Japan's colonies. Japan had military power to suppress. But did it 'fail'? It created national consciousness, exile government, martyrs.
Causes / Effects / Connections

Notes
• Read the Declaration of Independence aloud—it's eloquent and moving.
• An Jung-geun is hero in Korea, terrorist in Japan. Good example of contested historical memory.
• Yu Gwan-sun, a teenage girl tortured to death, is a major nationalist icon. Her story is powerful.
• March First is a national holiday in South Korea; understanding why helps understand Korean identity.
• Contemporary connection: Japan-Korea disputes often invoke this colonial history.
